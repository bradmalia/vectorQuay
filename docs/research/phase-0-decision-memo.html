Decision

Proceed with narrower scope. VectorQuay should move into Phase 1, but under a disciplined initial operating model shaped by the Phase 0 research findings.

Headline

VectorQuay is technically feasible on Coinbase Advanced spot, but current production-account economics make a roughly $47 to $50 starting balance fragile rather than comfortably viable.

What Was Confirmed

  • Coinbase Advanced Trade is the correct v1 API surface.
  • The approved trade-only, spot-only product direction is compatible with the chosen Coinbase API surface.
  • Authenticated read-only research works against both a temporary Coinbase account and the actual intended production account.
  • The actual production account currently reports pricing_tier = Intro 1, taker_fee_rate = 0.012, maker_fee_rate = 0.006, and total balance of about $47.26.
  • For the liquid major pairs examined, spreads are tight enough that fees matter more than spread at the current capital level.
  • The actual production account currently favors USD as the initial quote-currency path because a USD wallet is present while a USDC wallet was not observed in the returned account set at the time of inspection.

What This Means

Phase 0 Conclusion

VectorQuay is technically feasible and operationally coherent. The product should continue. However, the current confirmed fee state means the initial live operating model should be narrow, selective, and low-turnover rather than broad or aggressively high-frequency.

Economic Constraint

The production account’s confirmed Intro 1 fee tier makes the starting balance economically fragile. The main Phase 0 issue is no longer technical feasibility. It is whether the current fee burden is acceptable for the approved business goal at this capital level.

Default Exclusion Rule

All other assets remain excluded from the initial live trading universe unless later evidence shows they satisfy the approved quote-currency, tradability, market-quality, size, increment, and fee-viability rules.

Boundaries Preserved

  • All Coinbase-listed spot assets may be eligible for analysis in future phases.
  • Only a dynamically screened subset may be eligible for live trading.
  • Blockchain activity is preserved as a future signal-source research area.
  • MEV-style, mempool-priority, or miner and validator fee-bidding tactics remain out of scope for Coinbase Advanced spot execution.
  • No trade remains a valid system action if no asset passes screening.

Phase 1 Entry Recommendation

  • Proceed into Phase 1 using Coinbase Advanced only.
  • Keep v1 live-trading assumptions narrow: spot only, trade only, USD and USDC quotes only, with the approved 10-asset candidate class as the initial screening universe.
  • Design for dynamic asset discovery later, but do not enable all assets for live trading by default.
  • Assume low-turnover, maker-friendly behavior is more defensible than taker-heavy behavior at the current fee tier.
  • Preserve the option to revisit the effective capital floor later if aggressive growth remains the business priority.

Recommended Next Step

Begin Phase 1 PRD using this decision memo and the full Phase 0 findings as the implementation-planning baseline.
