Phase 4 PRD

Deterministic growth strategy, candidate scoring, and hard risk approval baseline.

Versionv0.1
StatusDraft baseline
OwnerBrad Malia
ThemeIndigo Signal

Executive Summary

Phase 4 turns VectorQuay from a read-only operator shell into a deterministic decision engine. It introduces a non-LLM growth-oriented strategy baseline, explicit trade-candidate scoring, hard risk approval rules, and auditable approval/rejection logic while still keeping live order placement and paper trading out of scope.

Phase Goal

Deliver a deterministic growth strategy and risk engine that can evaluate trade candidates safely, explain every approval or rejection, and prepare the product for later backtesting and paper-trading phases.

Purpose

Phase 3 established durable state, replayable audit history, and safe restart behavior. Phase 4 must now create the first real decisioning layer: a deterministic strategy core that can assess the approved asset universe, generate candidate trades, score them against growth criteria, and block unsafe actions through non-bypassable risk rules.

Carry-Forward Decisions

  • Coinbase Advanced Trade remains the only exchange surface.
  • Read-only exchange posture remains mandatory in Phase 4.
  • No live order placement, cancellation, or paper trading is introduced in this phase.
  • Only the approved/allowed asset universe may be considered for deterministic trade candidates.
  • Approved candidate overlays and protected-asset policies remain locally authoritative.
  • AI/LLM-driven signals remain out of scope until the deterministic baseline proves itself.

In Scope

  • Build a deterministic, non-LLM strategy baseline focused on growth rather than passive holding.
  • Separate signal generation, trade scoring, and risk approval into explicit pipeline stages.
  • Generate candidate trade actions from the approved asset universe using current Coinbase read-only state and current local policy overlays.
  • Implement configurable aggressiveness profiles that influence strategy thresholds but do not bypass hard limits.
  • Implement hard risk controls for position size, exposure, daily loss budget, turnover budget, stale data, connectivity failure, reconciliation mismatch, and operator kill switch posture.
  • Make approvals/rejections durable and explainable through the existing Phase 3 audit/history foundation.
  • Upgrade Activity and Performance so they can show deterministic candidate decisions in addition to historical account events.

Out of Scope

  • Live execution or order submission.
  • Paper trading on live market data.
  • Historical backtesting or replay qualification.
  • LLM, sentiment, watcher, or external-source intelligence scoring.
  • Advanced portfolio optimization beyond the deterministic candidate/risk model needed for this phase.

Primary Product Outcomes

  • The app can evaluate current account state and approved assets to produce deterministic candidate trade decisions.
  • Every candidate action is either approved or rejected through explicit, non-bypassable risk rules.
  • The operator can inspect why a candidate was approved, rejected, or downgraded.
  • Aggressive, balanced, and conservative behavior can be represented without weakening the hard safety boundary.
  • Phase 5 backtesting and Phase 6 paper trading can use the same deterministic decision pipeline rather than a throwaway prototype.

Operator Problems Solved

  • The operator can see what the system would want to do with the current account if execution were allowed.
  • The operator can verify that risk rules meaningfully constrain aggressive behavior.
  • The operator can review candidate trade rationale without trusting a black-box AI model.
  • The operator can compare the effect of different aggressiveness profiles before paper trading exists.

Decision Pipeline Requirements

  • Universe Filter: only approved and eligible assets enter candidate evaluation.
  • Signal Stage: deterministic price/account/state inputs create candidate direction and confidence inputs.
  • Scoring Stage: candidate trades receive a normalized deterministic score and rationale summary.
  • Risk Approval Stage: hard safety checks may reject or constrain candidates regardless of strategy score.
  • Decision Output Stage: final output must be one of at least: Approved, Rejected, Constrained, or No Action.

Hard Risk Controls

  • Maximum position size.
  • Maximum asset exposure / concentration.
  • Daily loss budget.
  • Turnover budget.
  • Protected-asset policy enforcement.
  • Stale data block.
  • Coinbase refresh/connectivity failure block.
  • Recovery/reconciliation mismatch block.
  • Operator kill-switch or inactive-trading block.

Aggressiveness Profiles

  • Phase 4 should support at minimum: Conservative, Balanced, and Aggressive.
  • Profiles may change scoring thresholds, candidate ranking sensitivity, and allowable trade frequency assumptions.
  • Profiles must not weaken non-bypassable hard limits.
  • The operator should be able to inspect which profile is active and how it affected the final decision.

Screen-Level Expectations

  • Overview: may surface top deterministic candidates and current strategy posture.
  • Activity: should become a mixed ledger of durable account events and deterministic candidate decisions, with clear type distinction.
  • Performance: should remain truthful and may begin showing simple decision-count / approval-rate summaries, but must not claim backtested alpha.
  • Risk & Thresholds: becomes the main operator surface for aggressiveness profile and hard-limit visibility.
  • Policies: remains authoritative for protected-asset and approved-universe constraints.
  • Alerts: may begin surfacing strategy/risk-related local alerts such as candidate rejection due to hard limits.

Functional Requirements

  • Implement deterministic candidate generation from current Coinbase read-only data and current local policy state.
  • Implement explicit candidate scoring that is explainable and reproducible from the same inputs.
  • Implement hard risk approval rules that cannot be bypassed by aggressiveness profile or high candidate score.
  • Persist decision outcomes and rationale through the Phase 3 durable-state foundation.
  • Render decision output in the GUI clearly enough for operator review.
  • Keep trading visibly inactive even when candidate trades are approved in theory.

Success Criteria

  • The product can produce deterministic candidate decisions from live read-only account data.
  • Hard risk rules reliably block unsafe or disallowed candidates.
  • Approval/rejection logic is auditable and explainable.
  • The implementation is stable enough to support Phase 5 backtesting against the same decision pipeline.

Phase 4 Is Complete When

  • Deterministic candidate generation and scoring are implemented.
  • Hard risk approval exists and is non-bypassable.
  • Decision outputs are durable and inspectable in the shell.
  • Phase 4 tests pass and the product is ready for Phase 5 backtesting and growth evaluation.

Risks

Phase 4 Risks

  • A weak deterministic baseline could produce plausible-looking but unsafe candidate decisions.
  • Profiles that are too loosely defined could undermine operator trust in the risk model.
  • Mixing strategy logic and hard-risk rules too early would make later qualification and debugging difficult.
