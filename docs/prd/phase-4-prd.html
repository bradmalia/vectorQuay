Product Requirements Document

Phase 4 PRD

Deterministic growth strategy, candidate scoring, and hard risk approval baseline.

Versionv0.1
StatusDraft baseline
OwnerBrad Malia
ThemeIndigo Signal

Executive Summary

Phase 4 turns VectorQuay from a read-only operator shell into a deterministic decision engine. It introduces a non-LLM growth-oriented strategy baseline, explicit trade-candidate scoring, hard risk approval rules, and auditable approval/rejection logic while still keeping live order placement and paper trading out of scope.

Phase Goal

Deliver a deterministic growth strategy and risk engine that can evaluate trade candidates safely, explain every approval or rejection, and prepare the product for later backtesting and paper-trading phases.

Purpose

Phase 3 established durable state, replayable audit history, and safe restart behavior. Phase 4 must now create the first real decisioning layer: a deterministic strategy core that can assess the approved asset universe, generate candidate trades, score them against growth criteria, and block unsafe actions through non-bypassable risk rules.

Initial Strategy Class

Phase 4 must implement one clearly defined deterministic strategy family rather than leaving strategy selection open-ended. The baseline strategy should be a trend-following / momentum deployment model that uses current portfolio state, current asset price state, and current approved-universe eligibility to identify buy, reduce, or no-action candidates. Mean-reversion, rotation, or multi-strategy blending are out of scope until a later phase explicitly approves them.

  • Primary objective: deploy capital toward assets with the strongest deterministic current-state trend posture inside the approved universe.
  • Secondary objective: avoid over-concentration, stale data, and churn that would obviously undermine growth after fees.
  • No external sentiment, watcher, or LLM-derived scoring is allowed in this phase.

Carry-Forward Decisions

  • Coinbase Advanced Trade remains the only exchange surface.
  • Read-only exchange posture remains mandatory in Phase 4.
  • No live order placement, cancellation, or paper trading is introduced in this phase.
  • Only the approved/allowed asset universe may be considered for deterministic trade candidates.
  • Approved candidate overlays and protected-asset policies remain locally authoritative.
  • AI/LLM-driven signals remain out of scope until the deterministic baseline proves itself.

In Scope

  • Build a deterministic, non-LLM strategy baseline focused on growth rather than passive holding.
  • Separate signal generation, trade scoring, and risk approval into explicit pipeline stages.
  • Generate candidate trade actions from the approved asset universe using current Coinbase read-only state and current local policy overlays.
  • Implement configurable aggressiveness profiles that influence strategy thresholds but do not bypass hard limits.
  • Implement hard risk controls for position size, exposure, daily loss budget, turnover budget, stale data, connectivity failure, reconciliation mismatch, and operator kill switch posture.
  • Make approvals/rejections durable and explainable through the existing Phase 3 audit/history foundation.
  • Upgrade Activity and Performance so they can show deterministic candidate decisions in addition to historical account events.

Out of Scope

  • Live execution or order submission.
  • Paper trading on live market data.
  • Historical backtesting or replay qualification.
  • LLM, sentiment, watcher, or external-source intelligence scoring.
  • Advanced portfolio optimization beyond the deterministic candidate/risk model needed for this phase.

Primary Product Outcomes

  • The app can evaluate current account state and approved assets to produce deterministic candidate trade decisions.
  • Every candidate action is either approved or rejected through explicit, non-bypassable risk rules.
  • The operator can inspect why a candidate was approved, rejected, or downgraded.
  • Aggressive, balanced, and conservative behavior can be represented without weakening the hard safety boundary.
  • Phase 5 backtesting and Phase 6 paper trading can use the same deterministic decision pipeline rather than a throwaway prototype.

Operator Problems Solved

  • The operator can see what the system would want to do with the current account if execution were allowed.
  • The operator can verify that risk rules meaningfully constrain aggressive behavior.
  • The operator can review candidate trade rationale without trusting a black-box AI model.
  • The operator can compare the effect of different aggressiveness profiles before paper trading exists.

Decision Pipeline Requirements

  • Universe Filter: only approved and eligible assets enter candidate evaluation.
  • Signal Stage: deterministic price/account/state inputs create candidate direction and confidence inputs.
  • Scoring Stage: candidate trades receive a normalized deterministic score and rationale summary.
  • Risk Approval Stage: hard safety checks may reject or constrain candidates regardless of strategy score.
  • Decision Output Stage: final output must be one of at least: Approved, Rejected, Constrained, or No Action.

Sell / Reduce Candidate Rules

  • Phase 4 must evaluate both deploy-capital and reduce-risk decisions.
  • Buy candidates represent capital deployment into an eligible approved asset.
  • Reduce candidates represent a deterministic recommendation to trim an existing position because trend posture weakened, concentration became too high, or a hard risk constraint requires exposure reduction.
  • Sell candidates should be limited to hard-risk or policy-driven exits in Phase 4 rather than free-form profit-taking logic.
  • Protected-asset and policy rules still govern whether a reduce or sell action may be recommended at all.

Asset Eligibility and Approval Contract

  • Phase 4 must distinguish asset eligibility from trade approval.
  • Asset eligibility determines whether an asset may enter the approved universe and candidate pipeline at all.
  • Trade approval determines whether an eligible asset may produce an Approved or Constrained trade candidate under current strategy and risk conditions.
  • Asset eligibility should remain primarily policy-governed and relatively stable; it must not be reclassified solely because of short-term scoring changes.
  • At minimum, an asset must satisfy all of the following before entering the approved universe:
    • listed and available on Coinbase spot
    • supported quote path through USD or USDC
    • not explicitly blocked by operator policy
    • passes minimum liquidity and spread requirements
    • not under a protected mode that fully forbids trading
  • Risk & Thresholds should influence trade approval, not the existence of the asset in the approved universe itself, except where a hard protected-asset rule fully forbids trading.
  • The system may recommend approval-state changes in later phases, but in Phase 4 it must not silently promote or demote assets without an explicit operator-visible explanation and durable record.
  • The operator may not manually force an ineligible asset into the approved universe through Phase 4 strategy controls; universe membership remains governed by policy and eligibility rules.

Evaluation Timing

  • Universe Eligibility Pass should run at startup after a successful Coinbase refresh, every time Refresh Universe is triggered, and whenever policy or approved-universe settings change.
  • Trade Candidate Evaluation Pass should run after each successful live refresh, after relevant policy/risk/profile changes, and whenever the operator explicitly requests reevaluation.
  • Neither pass should imply execution readiness or place orders.

Decision Explainability and User Validation

  • Every deterministic decision must be user-inspectable.
  • The operator must be able to review:
    • why the asset was eligible or excluded
    • the score breakdown and primary positive/negative factors
    • the hard-risk checks that passed, failed, or constrained the candidate
    • the final disposition: Approved, Rejected, Constrained, or No Action
    • the proposed side and any suggested size or size cap rationale
  • Decision explanations must persist through the Phase 3 audit/history foundation so the operator can validate prior decisions after restart.
  • The UI should make it obvious that these are deterministic strategy decisions, not executed account events.
  • Activity should be the primary decision-inspection surface in Phase 4.
  • Risk & Thresholds should be the primary surface for inspecting profile posture and hard-limit values that influenced the decision.
  • Overview may surface a compact decision summary, but it must not become the authoritative explanation surface.

Decision Retention Scope

  • Phase 4 should not persist a full No Action record for every evaluated asset on every pass.
  • Persistent decision history should include:
    • Approved decisions
    • Constrained decisions
    • Rejected decisions where the rejection reason is operator-relevant
    • top-ranked or operator-visible No Action cases only when they are needed to explain why nothing happened
  • This keeps Activity and audit history useful instead of flooding them with low-value non-decisions.

Hard Risk Controls

  • Maximum position size.
  • Maximum asset exposure / concentration.
  • Daily loss budget.
  • Turnover budget.
  • Protected-asset policy enforcement.
  • Stale data block.
  • Coinbase refresh/connectivity failure block.
  • Recovery/reconciliation mismatch block.
  • Operator kill-switch or inactive-trading block.

Aggressiveness Profiles

  • Phase 4 should support at minimum: Conservative, Balanced, and Aggressive.
  • Profiles may change scoring thresholds, candidate ranking sensitivity, and allowable trade frequency assumptions.
  • Profiles must not weaken non-bypassable hard limits.
  • The operator should be able to inspect which profile is active and how it affected the final decision.

Conservative should favor fewer, higher-confidence deployment candidates and stronger concentration control. Balanced should represent the default middle posture. Aggressive should surface more candidates and higher deployment willingness, but still remain inside the same hard safety limits.

Screen-Level Expectations

  • Overview: may surface top deterministic candidates and current strategy posture, but only as a compact summary.
  • Activity: should become a mixed ledger of durable account events and deterministic candidate decisions, with clear type distinction and an inspectable explanation view for decisions.
  • Performance: should remain truthful and may begin showing simple decision-count / approval-rate summaries such as approved, rejected, constrained, and no-action counts by asset and by profile, but must not claim backtested alpha.
  • Risk & Thresholds: becomes the main operator surface for aggressiveness profile and hard-limit visibility.
  • Policies: remains authoritative for protected-asset and approved-universe constraints.
  • Alerts: may begin surfacing strategy/risk-related local alerts such as candidate rejection due to hard limits.

Activity should remain the authoritative decision-inspection surface. Overview may summarize candidate posture, but it must not replace the detailed explanation workflow.

Evaluation Progress Visibility

  • The operator must be able to tell when deterministic evaluation is running.
  • The UI should expose at minimum these states: Idle, Refreshing Data, Evaluating Universe, Scoring Candidates, Applying Risk Checks, Completed, and Failed.
  • Progress visibility should appear in the footer/status bar and in any operator-triggered action surface such as Refresh Universe.
  • After evaluation completes, the shell should show a last-evaluated timestamp or equivalent completion cue.
  • Evaluation must not feel silent or stalled to the operator, even if the underlying work is relatively fast.

Functional Requirements

  • Implement a distinct universe-eligibility pass before trade candidate scoring begins.
  • Implement deterministic candidate generation from current Coinbase read-only data and current local policy state.
  • Implement explicit candidate scoring that is explainable and reproducible from the same inputs.
  • Implement deterministic sizing guidance for approved or constrained candidates, even if no orders are ever placed in this phase.
  • Implement hard risk approval rules that cannot be bypassed by aggressiveness profile or high candidate score.
  • Implement explicit decision explanation data so the operator can validate why the system made each choice.
  • Persist decision outcomes and rationale through the Phase 3 durable-state foundation.
  • Render decision output in the GUI clearly enough for operator review.
  • Keep trading visibly inactive even when candidate trades are approved in theory.

Success Criteria

  • The product can produce deterministic candidate decisions from live read-only account data.
  • Hard risk rules reliably block unsafe or disallowed candidates.
  • Approval/rejection logic is auditable and explainable.
  • Universe eligibility, scoring, and risk approval are visibly distinct to the operator.
  • Buy, reduce, and sell-side decisions are governed by explicit deterministic rules rather than implicit behavior.
  • The implementation is stable enough to support Phase 5 backtesting against the same decision pipeline.

Phase 4 Is Complete When

  • Deterministic candidate generation and scoring are implemented.
  • Hard risk approval exists and is non-bypassable.
  • Decision outputs are durable and inspectable in the shell.
  • Phase 4 tests pass and the product is ready for Phase 5 backtesting and growth evaluation.

Risks

Phase 4 Risks

  • A weak deterministic baseline could produce plausible-looking but unsafe candidate decisions.
  • Profiles that are too loosely defined could undermine operator trust in the risk model.
  • Mixing strategy logic and hard-risk rules too early would make later qualification and debugging difficult.
